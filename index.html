Variable Selection in Penalized Regression Models Using Distance Correlation

Completed

CSUSB Summer Research 2025 — Advanced research on variable selection techniques in high-dimensional predictive modeling. Successfully reduced 17,000+ predictors down to 20 without sacrificing accuracy by applying penalized regression with distance correlation, dramatically streamlining model complexity.

Key Achievement: Improved predictive performance by lowering MSE from 0.3255 to 0.2299 and optimizing AIC/BIC values through advanced regression modeling. Demonstrated that distance correlation outperforms traditional Pearson correlation by capturing nonlinear relationships, making it a more robust criterion for penalized regression frameworks. Ran large-scale simulations and data analyses in R/RStudio.

R RStudio Statistical Analysis Penalized Regression Distance Correlation Model Selection

Exoplanet Semi-Major Axis Prediction Model

Completed

CSUSB Summer Research 2024 — Interpreted NASA exoplanet datasets to develop a prediction model for missing data on the semi-major axis of exoplanets. Used R/RStudio for extensive data manipulation and creation of advanced statistical models.

Results: Significantly improved the model with log transformation techniques, raising the R-squared to 99% and reducing RMSE to 0.162. Applied advanced data science methodologies to uncover insights from astronomical datasets and handle missing data patterns.
